        <h2>About Me</h2>

        <p>Dr. Qiaoli Wu, female, Lecturer, born in September 1992, from Jingzhou, Hubei Province, China. She is a member of the Communist Party of China. She obtained her Ph.D. in Cartography and Geographic Information System from the Faculty of Geographical Science, Beijing Normal University, with joint training at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, and was a visiting scholar at the University of California, Berkeley. Her research focuses on remote sensing of vegetation ecology, including gross primary productivity estimation, remote sensing mechanism modeling, forest growth modeling, carbon-water coupling models, and ecological effects assessment of major national restoration projects. She has led or participated in eight national research projects, including the Ministry of Science and Technology’s Key R&D Program, 973 Program, 863 Program, and National Natural Science Foundation projects. As the first author, she has published over ten SCI, EI, and core journal papers and has received multiple awards.</p>
